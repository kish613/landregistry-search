<h2>Start with the company number when you have it</h2>
<p>The cleanest way to check corporate ownership is to begin with the <strong>Companies House registration number</strong>. That avoids false positives caused by name changes, abbreviations, or similarly named group entities. Once you have the number, screen it against HM Land Registry CCOD data and review the titles returned.</p>

<h3>Fast due-diligence workflow</h3>
<ol>
  <li>Confirm the entity at Companies House and note its registration number.</li>
  <li>Run a company-number search in the registry tool.</li>
  <li>Review returned titles, tenure, and proprietor details.</li>
  <li>Cross-check with company records and escalate to title documents where the matter is material.</li>
</ol>

<h3>What this check can answer</h3>
<p>This workflow helps determine whether a company appears in the corporate ownership dataset, whether ownership seems concentrated in one region, and whether related address or title details justify a deeper legal review.</p>
