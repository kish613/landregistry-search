How to search corporate land ownership efficiently

Use company number when possible, company name when needed, and address search when the site is already known. The goal is to move quickly from a research question into a focused diligence workflow.

Step 1

Validate the entity

Check the legal entity first and compare the workflow with Companies House vs Land Registry if you are unsure which source answers which question.

Step 2

Run the best-fit search type

Use company number, company name, director, or address based on what you know. If you need a primer, start with Find Properties Owned by a Company.

Step 3

Escalate only the material matches

Once titles or sites surface, use the results to decide whether the matter needs formal title review, further legal work, or a portfolio-level export.
